Chapter 3 - Forces Simulation

Forces can be added and subtracted to give a resulting net force. To understand the basics of calculating net force, let us consider a game of tug-of-war.

In tug-of-war, players form two teams on opposite ends of a rope and pull against each other until one team is pulled over the centre line. Or put it another way, each team exerts force in opposite directions, and the team with the greatest combined force will win because the net force is going in their direction.

Example:

Team 1 consists of two people capable pulling to the left 9 N and 15 N respectively. Team 2 has three people who can pull 10 N 11 N and 6 N to the left. To figure out which team will win, calculate the net force by adding up the forces of each team.

For team 1, the total force is 9 N + 15 N = 24 N. For team 2, the total force is 10 N + 11 N + 6 N = 27 N.

Net force is 24 N subtract 27 N (we are subtracting 27 N because team 2 is pulling in the opposite direction of team 1). Therefore the total force is -3 N, which means Team 2 wins.

( 9 + 15 ) - ( 10 + 11 + 6 ) = -3 N
